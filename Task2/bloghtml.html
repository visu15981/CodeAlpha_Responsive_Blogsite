What is Virtual Reality

Virtual Reality (VR) is a computer-generated simulation or recreation of a real or imaginary environment that can be experienced and interacted with by a person. It immerses the user in a three-dimensional environment, typically through the use of a head-mounted display (HMD) and other input devices.
The primary goal of VR is to create a sense of presence, where the user feels as if they are physically present in the virtual environment. This is achieved by stimulating the user's visual, auditory, and sometimes even tactile senses to create a convincing and immersive experience.

AR

What is Augumented Reality

AR stands for Augmented Reality. It is a technology that overlays virtual objects or information onto the real world, blending digital content with the user's real-world environment. Unlike Virtual Reality (VR), which creates a fully simulated virtual environment, AR enhances the real world by adding computer-generated elements. AR technology typically relies on a camera-equipped device, such as a smartphone or tablet, to capture the real-world view. The device's sensors and software then analyze the captured image or video to identify and track objects.
